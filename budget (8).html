Every dollar
gets a job.

The fundamental principle is this: every dollar you earn gets assigned a job before you spend it. Not after. Not "let's see how much is left at the end of the month." Before. When your paycheck lands, you decide where it goes — rent, groceries, gas, savings — until every dollar has a destination. That's it. That's the whole idea.

Why this works

Most people budget backwards. They spend freely, then check their bank account and wonder where it all went. Zero-based budgeting flips that. You're not tracking the past — you're planning the future, one month at a time.

The mental shift is from "I have $3,000 in my account, I'm fine" to "I have $3,000, and here's exactly where all of it is going." Those two sentences feel similar but produce completely different outcomes.
